Your Privacy Protection Guide
A clean result is a good starting point — here's how to stay that way.
Tip 01
Lock your SIM with a carrier PIN
SIM-swap attacks let scammers take over your number by porting it to a new SIM. Set a SIM lock PIN with your carrier — call Jio, Airtel, Vi or BSNL customer care to enable it.
Tip 02
Use app-based 2FA instead of SMS 2FA
SMS-based one-time passwords can be intercepted through SIM-swap attacks. Switch to app-based 2FA using Google Authenticator or Authy wherever possible — it's far more secure.
Tip 03
Never share OTPs — even with "bank" or "customer service" callers
No legitimate bank, wallet, or app will ever ask for your OTP. If anyone calls asking for an OTP or PIN — it's a scam. Hang up and call the official number on your bank's website instead.
Tip 04
Remove your number from Truecaller
Truecaller exposes your name and number to millions of users. Visit truecaller.com/unlisting and request removal. It takes ~24 hours but removes you from one of the largest phone directories in India.
Tip 05
Use a separate number for app signups
Get a second SIM or use a virtual number (like Doosra) for food delivery, shopping and random signups. Keep your main number private — only share it with people you trust.
Tip 06
Watch for phishing calls and smishing texts
Scammers buy leaked phone lists. If you get a call or SMS claiming to be from your bank, a courier, or a "KYC team" — don't click links or share details. Go directly to the official app instead.
Tip 07
Scan again in 90 days
Phone leaks are often discovered months after they happen. A clean result today doesn't guarantee clean results tomorrow. Book your next scan at scanmyshadow.com.
