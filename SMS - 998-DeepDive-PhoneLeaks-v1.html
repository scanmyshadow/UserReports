Your Privacy Protection Guide
Steps to take right now to reduce your exposure.
Tip 01
Change your email password immediately
Your email is the master key to all your accounts. Change it now — at least 12 characters, unique, not used anywhere else.
Tip 02
Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)
Enable 2FA on Gmail or Outlook — even with your password leaked, hackers cannot access your account without a second verification step. Use an authenticator app, not SMS.
Tip 03
Change passwords on all breached platforms
Start with the platforms listed in the breach section above. If you reused your email password anywhere, change it there too — credential stuffing attacks are common.
Tip 04
Watch for phishing emails and fake calls
Scammers buy leaked email data. If you receive an email claiming to be from your bank or an app asking you to log in — go directly to the website instead of clicking any link.
Tip 05
Check which apps have access to your Google account
Go to myaccount.google.com → Security → Third-party apps. Remove anything you do not recognise or no longer use. These apps can read your email and contacts.
Tip 06
Use a separate email for app sign-ups
Create a second email address just for registrations and app sign-ups. Keep your main email private — only share it with people and services you fully trust.
Tip 07
Set up a password manager
Use a password manager (like Bitwarden or 1Password) so each account has its own strong, unique password — making future leaks far less damaging.
Tip 08
Scan again in 90 days
Breaches are often discovered months after they happen. A clean result today does not guarantee clean results tomorrow. Book your next scan at scanmyshadow.com.
